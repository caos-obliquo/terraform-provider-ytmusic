4	Emetic Cult
Haemorrhage
Emetic Cult (1995)

'Foetal Mincer' (2:21)

If you know the old Carcass worship sound, you know what this. Haemorrhage has been plugging away at the style for years and years and this is where it all began, with shaky blast beats, gurgling vocals, and deathy grindy punky riffs left and right. It's not dramatically developed music in any sense; the production is good but still amateurish, instrumental performances are off and on in quality, and the songwriting is catchy but far from stellar. There's the usual suspects of tremolo riffs versus those that are more or less chuggy or punky, the occasional d-beat, and a certain noisiness to everything going on.

The songwriting is good. Haemorrhage probably stole 90% of the riffs on this album but it doesn't really matter because they never claimed to be original. The songs are catchy if not particularly memorable on their own and the spastic high/low/everywhere in between vocals are pretty cool. If you were simply told what Haemorrhage sounds like, what you hear in your head would be this album: enthusiastic, unprofessional, and surprisingly good despite its amateurish aims. It's not the sort of music that will convert a power metal fan to the death/grind scene, but it's a fun listen every once in a while. Haemorrhage excels at making short, catchy songs, and this album has fourteen of them for your listening pleasure.

If you like Haemorrhage or Carcassy deathgrind, just buy it already. Then again, if you're in either of those audiences, you probably already own it. By another copy of it anyway, I don't know, get the hell away from me. Noktorn

As far as Carcass influenced (plagiarising is perhaps more precise) gore grind goes Pathologist were among the best. 6	A Chapter of Accidents
Dead Infection
A Chapter of Accidents (1995)

'Airplanes Catastrophe' 2:35

Despite how much sampling lends itself to Goregrind, sometimes they need to be stripped away to give a band a sense of bluntness and filth only replicated by 'red rooms' of the dark web.

The production on this album could almost be considered the benchmark for production for future bands to lunge themselves towards, Blastbeats stripping bodies of flesh;
Bass as thick as a brick,
Guitars as heavy as the stereotypical Goregrind listener.

Now, without using any hyperbole, this is one of the most straight-forward goregrind releases out there. Some of the guitar lines are reminiscent of Scum-era Napalm Death with their sloppy grooves. I also like the whole theme behind the lyrics, of accidents gone gory.
7	The Encyclopedia of Serial Murders
Catasexual Urge Motivation
The Encyclopedia of Serial Murders (1996)

'Vivid Stains in Hematomania's Delirium' (4:21)

Now in comparison to the endless Carcass worship that runs sloppily through Goregrind, CUM here brings in elements from Industrial and occasionanly turns down the tempo to Death-Doom throughout the 22 tracks. The drum machine is used rather well in this release, with the drum samples having more of an electronic basis rather than a metal base. Despite the electronic elements, this still remains an proper gorefest. The concept of the album appears to cover killing and the psychology behind such acts with titles like ' Mass Murder, the Only Way to Become God' and 'Murder Is Better Than Birth'.
Those poetic descriptions of death I believ tie this release up with the 'Guinea Pig' films that were made in Japan during the mid-80s.

14	Olidous Operettas

'Casper’s Dictum' (4:12)

Carcass copycatism done by a trio of legit doctors. The music in here is no joke however: this is pretty damn legit deathgrind, it's like the direct sequel that Symphonies of Sickness never had, with slightly worse riffs and a more clear production. While this is an unabashed ripoff and doesn't even try to cover it up, it elevates plagiarism to the point of art - it's done so well and true to the original that I can only give these guys a big thumbs up, since this could probably even pass for a Carcass full-length (and then Carcass would get accused of recycling their own riffs). It's like watching somebody repaint Mona Lisa in front of your eyes.

Oh yeah, and the cover art is a scratch 'n' sniff that smells of rotten meat. If that's not awesome, then I don't know what is. ~nightwrath

20	Putrefaction in Progress
Last Days of Humanity
Putrefaction in Progress (2006)

'A Divine Proclamation of Finishing the Present Existence' (3:11)


Many consider goregrind as sort of an in-between stage between the development of grindcore into deathgrind. Which when taking into account the rate at which the genre has over the past 10 years been reduced to a secondary genre tag or sharing the main genre tag with deathgrind, does seem like fact. The genre itself has ground to a halt in terms of innovation. While I'm sure people will still worship Carcass and LDOS until we die, in terms of innovation (drum machines, pitch shifting) we might have reached the limit, short of the record snuffing the listener. The only way forward in that regard is with better tech, better drum machines, etc.

In that case, to wrap up this box set, we need a final defining album to round off this list. Something that takes all of the goregrind elements (pitch-shifted vocals, quick drum playing, filthy bass) and just removes all subtlety, all appeals to anything melodic, just rhythms breaking dimensional barriers and guitars tuned so low, they sound like an amplified bass.

In other words, here's Putrefaction in Process, enjoy!
